# VAI Business Model — Source of Truth

_The financial and ownership spine of VAI. Companion to
[master-plan.md](../plans/master-plan.md) (the product "why") and
[architecture.md](../plans/architecture.md) (how it's built). This doc answers the
business questions the product docs deliberately leave out: **what VAI earns, what
it's worth, what Brianne is worth, and how other people earn a share.** The entity
paperwork, coach agreements, licensing proposals, and any investor conversation all
draw from this doc._

_Last updated: 2026-07-20. Figures marked **(illustrative)** are models to react

## 1. What VAI actually is

VAI is **a coaching business wrapped around a proprietary measurement instrument.**
That framing drives every number below:

- **The coaching** is the *cash engine* — it pays the bills but is capped by
  delivery time (today, Brianne's).
- **The instrument** (the Ownership Debt Assessment — a validated,
  real-estate-specific diagnostic that produces *score-movement proof over time*)
  is the *equity engine* — IP, the moat, and the thing that can earn revenue
  **decoupled from anyone's hours**.

Almost everything in today's product roadmap invests in the cash engine. The
equity engine — the part that makes VAI *worth something* rather than *earn
something* — is the strategic frontier this doc exists to protect.

### The three layers

| Layer | What it is | Revenue character | Ceiling |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **L1 — You coach** | Done-With-You, Brianne delivers solo | High price, 100% her time | ~$300–500k/yr gross, then full **(illustrative)** |
| **L2 — Coaches coach** | Certified/contract coaches deliver; VAI takes an override | Recurring, partial margin | $1–3M/yr **(illustrative)** |
| **L3 — Instrument licenses** | Brokerages/franchises pay to *use* the assessment as their coaching/ops backbone | Recurring SaaS, near-zero COGS | The valuation multiplier |

**Decided direction (2026-07-20):** L3 is aimed at **large brands and franchises
— RE/MAX, Keller Williams, eXp, Compass — not primarily WSS.** WSS is a possible
licensee but not the priority; selling the instrument outside WSS both enlarges
the opportunity and removes the family entanglement. Enterprise buyers demand a
credible standalone company, which is why the owned-pipeline work (§6, B4) matters.

## 2. The financial model

### The P&L shape (this structure is the whole answer)

```
  Gross revenue  (coaching + licensing)
– Processing fees (~3%)
– WSS split       (only on bounded founding seats — see §5)
─────────────────────────────
= Net revenue
– COGS: coach delivery comp   ← includes BRIANNE's coaching, paid at market
─────────────────────────────
= Gross profit
– Opex: operator salary ($10k/mo), tools, marketing, bookkeeping
─────────────────────────────
= Operating profit
– Cash reserve (2–3 mo opex)
─────────────────────────────
= Distributable profit → DIVIDENDS (pro-rata to vested equity)
```

The load-bearing line is **"COGS: coach delivery comp — includes Brianne's
coaching, paid at market."** Everything in §3–4 depends on treating delivery as a
real cost, even when Brianne is the one delivering.

### Near-term cash — founding launch (illustrative)

Pricing is **decided** (see [dormant-reactivation pitch](dormant-reactivation-pitch-to-wss.md)):
founding seat = **$997/mo × 3 = $2,991**, cap 10; standard rate **$1,997/mo**.

- 8 of 10 founding seats fill @ $2,991 = **$23,928 gross (illustrative fill)**
- If WSS-sourced, 60/40 split (decided, bounded): **VAI $14,357 / WSS $9,571**
- Say 5 founders continue at their locked $997/mo = **$4,985/mo recurring**
- Website-sourced clients ramp at $1,997/mo as the owned front door goes live

This is a real business starting — but in solo mode it's a **job, not an asset**,
because it's entirely Brianne's time.

### Why "solo + $10k + dividends" is a story, not economics

Imagine Brianne coaches ~12 clients, pays herself a $10k operator salary, sweeps
the rest as a "dividend" (illustrative):

| Line | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| 12 clients × ~$1,500 blended | **$18,000/mo** |
| – Operator salary | –$10,000 |
| – Opex | –$2,500 |
| = "Dividend" | **≈ $5,500/mo** |

Looks like salary + dividend. But she *delivered all 12 herself.* A contract coach
doing that work is worth ~50% of revenue ≈ $9,000/mo. The honest P&L:

| Line | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Revenue | $18,000 |
| – Delivery (her coaching at market) | –$9,000 |
| – Operator salary | –$10,000 |
| = True founder profit | **≈ –$3,500** |

**In solo mode the business isn't throwing off a dividend — she's paying herself
~$15.5k across three jobs and *calling* $5.5k of it a dividend.** Fine as income;
fatal as a story, because the moment a coach or investor runs diligence they re-cut
it exactly this way and the "profit" disappears. Telling the truth now is what makes
the partnership model (§4) credible.

**Real dividends require leverage, and there are exactly two sources:**
1. **Someone else delivers** (a coach gives *time*) → VAI earns an override *above*
   Brianne's own wages.
2. **The instrument earns without delivery** (L3 licensing) → gross-margin revenue
   that is nobody's hours.

That is the entire game: **leverage, then license.**

## 3. The opportunity

- **Market:** 100k+ real estate teams in the US; the L3 franchises (RE/MAX, KW,
  eXp, Compass) collectively touch hundreds of thousands of agents. A niche
  coaching practice serving even 200–300 team leaders at ~$1.5–2k/mo is a
  **$5–8M/yr** business **(illustrative)**.
- **Solo (L1):** ~$300–500k/yr gross, but ~$150–250k of that is really Brianne's
  combined wages. Excellent income, **zero enterprise value** — it dies if she stops.
- **Coach-leveraged (L2):** $1–3M/yr, with an override margin that is *actual*
  profit. Starts to have enterprise value because it survives her.
- **Instrument-as-platform (L3):** the multiplier. Recurring license revenue at
  high margin is valued at **3–8× ARR** on exit. A VAI at ~$1M recurring (coaching
  + licenses) at ~30% margin could be a **$3–4M+ enterprise (illustrative)** — the
  number where founder equity becomes wealth, not wages.

**The potential is not the coaching revenue. It's owning the instrument a coaching
organization — or a national brokerage — runs on.**

## 4. What Brianne is worth, and how others earn a share

### Three checks, not one

| Role | What it's worth | How it's paid | Note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Operator / CEO** | Market $100–180k/yr | **Salary — the $10k/mo (decided intent)** | Early on, tie to cash flow: draw what the business supports, accrue the rest |
| **Coach / delivery** | ~40–60% of the coaching revenue she personally delivers | **Coach fee (COGS)** | The check currently hidden inside "dividends." Pay it explicitly. |
| **Founder / owner** | The residual + enterprise value | **Dividends (pro-rata equity) + eventual sale** | The real wealth. Keep it honest so it stays credible. |

Paying all three is what makes VAI *legible* to a coach ("here's your 50%") and to
a buyer ("here's the true margin"). Conflating them is the single biggest thing
that sabotages bringing other people in.

### The cap table — "give time or money" (build for it now)

**Decided (2026-07-20):** partners are real and near-term — build the structure
now rather than retrofitting.

**Time-partners (coaches) — the ones VAI most wants.** They earn:
- **Now:** rev-share on clients they deliver (e.g., 50/50 of net) — variable, tied
  to production, no downside to VAI.
- **Over time:** a vesting track into a capped, **non-voting profits-interest pool**
  (e.g., up to ~10% total, split among coach-partners) — vest by delivering ≥N
  clients for ≥12 months at a quality bar. Upside for *building*, not just wages;
  locks in the best coaches. They risk time.

**Money-partners (investors).** Buy equity at a set valuation (or a SAFE if too
early to price), no delivery role, pro-rata dividends + exit upside. Keep small and
optional — the coaching business is capital-light. Money is worth taking mainly to
(a) fund owned marketing that reduces WSS dependency, or (b) build the L3 platform.
Otherwise bootstrap and keep equity whole.

> **Recommendation:** time-partners are worth far more to VAI than money-partners.
> Recruit coaches before raising a dollar — money can't deliver coaching; coaches
> are how you escape the solo ceiling and start throwing real dividends.

### Fairness / governance guardrails

- **Salaries and coach fees are expenses, paid *before* any dividend.** Everyone is
  paid for work; owners split what's *left*. Keeps the math honest, protects every
  party.
- **Dividends only from retained profit after a 2–3 month cash reserve.**
- **Dividends pro-rata to *vested* equity only** — a coach on rev-share who hasn't
  vested doesn't also draw dividends (no double-dip).
- **Brianne keeps control:** >50% equity + decision rights; the coach pool is
  non-voting profits interest.
- **Entity:** an **LLC taxed as an S-corp** is the textbook fit for "reasonable
  salary + distributions" — literally the "$10k salary then dividends" structure,
  and tax-efficient; profits-interest pools are cleanest in an LLC. Paper it with a
  CPA + attorney. (This doc lays out the standard shape; it is not legal/tax advice.)

### The dividend waterfall once leverage exists (illustrative)

20 active clients; Brianne delivers 12, one contract coach delivers 8:

| Line | Amount |
| --- | --- |
| Gross (20 × ~$1,500) | $30,000 |
| – Processing (~3%) | –$900 |
| – Coach delivery COGS (both @ 50%) | –$15,000 |
| = Gross profit | $14,100 |
| – Operator salary (Brianne) | –$10,000 |
| – Opex | –$2,500 |
| = Operating profit → reserve/dividend | **≈ $1,600** |

Still thin — because at this scale VAI pays the operator salary *and* both coach
fees. **The dividend only fattens as coach count grows past Brianne's own delivery,
and as L3 licensing (near-zero COGS) comes online.** The waterfall makes the
strategy self-evident.

## 5. The WSS relationship (balanced posture)

**Decided (2026-07-20): balanced — WSS is launch fuel; VAI's owned pipeline builds
in parallel.** WSS supplies the warm dormant list, brand trust (Verl's name), and
top-of-funnel — cheap, warm traffic to launch the founding cohort. But concentration
on WSS is also the thing that would make VAI *a WSS feature rather than a company* —
and enterprise L3 buyers (RE/MAX et al.) need to see a credible standalone brand.

So: use WSS fully to fund the early business, and steadily build VAI's owned funnel
(website + nurture) so dependency falls over time without starving either. The
[dormant-reactivation split](dormant-reactivation-pitch-to-wss.md) stays **bounded**
(60/40 on founding WSS-sourced seats only) precisely so WSS is paid for the
reactivation it enables, not for VAI's business in perpetuity.

## 6. What's missing to build the *business* (not just the app) — critique

Today's roadmap builds a **tool** well and a **business** not at all. Gaps:

1. **Delivery is 100% Brianne, and nothing on the roadmap changes it.** The
   coach-facing views (product theme 3) are framed as *internal tooling*, not the
   delivery surface for a *coach network*. The leverage that creates dividends
   isn't planned.
2. **The instrument's licensing value (L3) is unmonetized.** The biggest asset has
   no revenue model attached. Building the equity engine and giving it away is the
   central miss — now sharpened by the decision to license to national brands.
3. **No revenue tracking anywhere.** "Purchase is a booking, not a transaction" —
   all revenue is off-platform and manual; attribution for the WSS split is already
   an *open risk*. Without a per-client / per-coach / per-source ledger, splits,
   coach comp, and dividends are uncomputable. Foundational to the partnership model.
4. **WSS concentration risk** — mitigated by the balanced posture above, but only
   if the owned-pipeline work actually happens.
5. **No business infrastructure** — no entity, cap table, banking, or bookkeeping.
   The literal prerequisite for "other people involved."

### The added track — Business Operations (B0–B4)

Tracked in
[plans/tasks/20260720-business-operations-track.md](../plans/tasks/20260720-business-operations-track.md).
Given "build for partners now," B0 and B1 are near-term.

- **B0 — Entity & money plumbing.** LLC → S-corp election, business bank account,
  bookkeeping, define the $10k operator draw + Brianne's coach fee. The "others can
  join" unlock.
- **B1 — Revenue ledger.** Bring the **dormant `clients` table** alive: link
  session → client → subscription → revenue, tagged by source (WSS vs. owned) and
  coach. Makes splits, comp, and dividends computable; makes VAI legible to a
  partner or buyer.
- **B2 — Coach leverage (L2).** First capacity ceiling → certify coach #1 on 50/50
  rev-share, delivering through the coach-facing admin views (theme 3 repurposed as
  *product*, not internal tooling). First real dividend; template for the pool.
- **B3 — Enterprise licensing (L3).** Package the instrument as a licensable product
  and open conversations with brokerages/franchises (RE/MAX, KW, eXp, Compass). The
  valuation multiplier.
- **B4 — Owned pipeline.** Website + nurture as the standalone funnel — the WSS-
  independence hedge and the credibility L3 buyers require.

## 7. Recommended order

1. **Run the WSS dormant test; fill the founding cohort** (in flight) — cheapest
   warm traffic, already built.
2. **Stand up B0 + B1 immediately** — entity, bank, bookkeeping, revenue ledger.
   The gate to every partnership and dividend. Do it before promising anyone a split.
3. **Pay all three checks from day one**, even if two accrue unpaid early — keeps
   the numbers honest.
4. **Recruit coach #1 (time-partner) at the first capacity ceiling** — first real
   dividend, template for the pool.
5. **Package L3 and open enterprise-license conversations** once the cohort produces
   score-movement proof — the valuation multiplier.
6. **Build the owned pipeline in parallel** (balanced posture) — independence +
   enterprise credibility.
7. **Take money only if it buys independence or accelerates L3** — otherwise
   bootstrap and keep equity.
