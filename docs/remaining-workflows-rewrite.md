## Section D - Seller Communication
### Weekly updates, feedback, and price review prep

### Financial Accountability (weight 0.20 total, 0.07 per question)

Q019 When a seller pushes back on feedback or has a question that's outside their lane, can they navigate that moment and get to the right next step, whether that means handling it directly or routing it to you, without you having to manage it personally?

Q020 Do they send the weekly update straight to the seller, or does it come across your desk for review first?

Q021 If a seller gets upset about a scheduling issue or a communication gap, do they smooth that over directly, or does it end up needing your involvement?

### Role Accountability (weight 0.22 total, 0.07 per question)

Q022 In the last 30 days, how often did they handle a tough seller conversation on their own, like a stalled showing schedule or a seller frustrated about turnaround time?

Q023 When a seller reaches out with a concern between updates, who responds first?

Q024 When a seller starts asking why there's been no activity after weeks on market, do they bring you a plan for addressing it, like refreshed marketing or updated photos, or do they ask you how to handle it?

### Systems and Checklists (weight 0.18 total, 0.06 per question)

Q025 Is there a set process for the weekly seller update, like when it goes out and what it covers, that they actually follow?

Q026 Are the weekly update and feedback summary templated, or written from scratch each time?

Q027 If a seller conversation gets difficult, like frustration over a lack of showings, do they have talking points or a guide to work from, or do they call you first?

### Execution and Training (weight 0.15 total, 0.05 per question)

Q028 Do they know which seller conversations are theirs to handle versus which ones need you on the call?

Q029 In the last 60 days, did they bring you a seller conversation that they could have handled themselves?

Q030 If they were unavailable for a few days, is there someone who could step in on seller communication without the seller noticing a gap?

### Outcome Accountability (weight 0.15 total, 0.05 per question)

Q031 If a seller relationship sours, whether from poor communication or unmet expectations, who's accountable to you for that?

Q032 Do they have a real standard for seller communication, not just "did the update go out" but "does the seller feel informed and confident"?

Q033 If seller satisfaction keeps coming up short under their watch, is there an actual process for addressing that?

### Culture and Communication (weight 0.10 total, 0.03 per question)

Q034 How confident are you that they can manage the full day-to-day seller relationship, including tense moments about scheduling or communication gaps, without you?

Q035 Based on how they actually handle sellers, not just what they report back, how confident do they seem?

Q036 Have they been running seller communication independently, at your standard, for 90 days or more?

## Section E - File Opening
### From executed contract to critical date calendar

### Financial Accountability (weight 0.20 total, 0.07 per question)

Q037 From the contract being executed to the critical date calendar being built, can they set it up and move forward without you signing off first?

Q038 When the file needs to go to title, escrow, or the other agent with key dates and terms, do they send that directly?

Q039 If a critical date gets entered wrong or a document is missing at file opening, do they catch and fix it, or does it surface later as your problem?

### Role Accountability (weight 0.22 total, 0.07 per question)

Q040 In the last 30 days, how often did they open a file and build out the critical dates without checking with you first?

Q041 When something's off with a new file, like a missing signature or an unclear deadline, who catches it first?

Q042 When a file opening issue comes up, like a document discrepancy, do they bring you a fix, or do they bring you the problem?

### Systems and Checklists (weight 0.18 total, 0.06 per question)

Q043 Is there a documented checklist for opening a file and building the critical date calendar that they actually use?

Q044 Are the file opening confirmations and critical date notices templated, or written fresh each time?

Q045 If a file has something unusual, like extra documentation needed for a nonstandard contingency, do they know how to flag it and gather what's needed, or do they call you?

### Execution and Training (weight 0.15 total, 0.05 per question)

Q046 Do they know which file opening decisions are theirs versus which ones need your review?

Q047 In the last 60 days, did they escalate a file opening question to you that was within their own authority to answer?

Q048 If they were out for a day, could someone else pick up file opening without dates slipping?

### Outcome Accountability (weight 0.15 total, 0.05 per question)

Q049 If a critical date gets missed because a file wasn't opened correctly, who's accountable to you for that?

Q050 Do they have a clear standard for file opening, not just "was it opened" but "was every date and detail correct"?

Q051 If file opening errors keep happening under their watch, is there a real process for correcting that?

### Culture and Communication (weight 0.10 total, 0.03 per question)

Q052 How confident are you that they can open a file and build a critical date calendar correctly without your review?

Q053 Based on their actual track record, not just their explanations, how confident do they seem handling file opening on their own?

Q054 Have they been opening files independently, error-free at your standard, for 90 days or more?

## Section F - Lender Tracking
### Appraisal, underwriting, CTC, and closing coordination

### Financial Accountability (weight 0.20 total, 0.07 per question)

Q055 From the appraisal being ordered through clear-to-close and coordinating the actual closing, can they manage that without checking in with you at each step?

Q056 When lender updates need to go out, like an appraisal result or a CTC confirmation, do they send those directly to the parties involved?

Q057 If something gets missed in lender tracking, like a stale underwriting update or a closing detail, do they catch and resolve it themselves?

### Role Accountability (weight 0.22 total, 0.07 per question)

Q058 In the last 30 days, how often did they follow up with the lender and move a file forward without you asking them to?

Q059 When a lender issue comes up, like a delayed appraisal or a stalled underwriting file, who follows up first?

Q060 When a lending delay threatens the closing date, do they bring you options for rescheduling logistics, like updated timing with title and the other side's office, or do they ask you what to do?

### Systems and Checklists (weight 0.18 total, 0.06 per question)

Q061 Is there a documented process for tracking a file from appraisal through closing that they actually follow?

Q062 Are lender status updates and closing confirmations templated, or written fresh each time?

Q063 If a lending issue is unusual, like a delayed underwriting condition, do they have a playbook for gathering what's needed and notifying the right people, or do they call you?

### Execution and Training (weight 0.15 total, 0.05 per question)

Q064 Do they know which lender or closing issues are theirs to resolve versus which ones need you involved?

Q065 In the last 60 days, did they escalate a lender tracking issue to you that they could have resolved on their own?

Q066 If they were unavailable for a day near closing, could someone else keep the file moving with the lender?

### Outcome Accountability (weight 0.15 total, 0.05 per question)

Q067 If a closing gets delayed or falls through due to a lender tracking miss, who's accountable to you for that?

Q068 Do they have a real standard for lender tracking, not just "did they follow up" but "did the file actually close on time"?

Q069 If closing delays keep happening under their watch, is there a real process for correcting that?

### Culture and Communication (weight 0.10 total, 0.03 per question)

Q070 How confident are you that they can manage a file from appraisal to closing without your involvement?

Q071 Based on how they've actually handled lender tracking, not just what they tell you, how confident do they seem?

Q072 Have they been managing lender tracking and closings independently, at your standard, for 90 days or more?
