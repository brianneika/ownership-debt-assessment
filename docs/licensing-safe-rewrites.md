# Ownership Debt Assessment - Licensing-Safe Rewrites

These 12 questions replace the flagged versions from the Listing Launch and Remaining
Workflows rewrite docs. Same dimension, same weight, same 0 to 4 scale and direction as
before. Only the underlying action changed, from something that requires a license to
something legally safe for anyone, licensed or not.

No new question, no license-status field, no state logic, nothing added to the assessment
flow. These just work correctly for every respondent from here on.

---

## Section C - Listing Launch

**Q002** (Financial Accountability) Manus flag: Depends
Before: When they need to send pricing updates, marketing plans, or MLS confirmations to your seller, do they send those directly, or do you review everything before it goes out?
After: When they need to send status updates, marketing plans, or MLS confirmations to your seller, do they send those directly, or do you review everything before it goes out?

**Q005** (Role Accountability) Manus flag: Depends
Before: When a seller calls with a last-minute change, like a price drop, new photos, or a showing conflict, who fields that call first?
After: When a seller calls with a last-minute change, like new photos needed or a showing conflict, who fields that call first?

**Q009** (Systems and Checklists) Manus flag: Restricted
Before: If something unusual comes up, like a title issue, a seller disagreement on price, or a vendor falling through, do they have something to reference, or do they call you?
After: If something unusual comes up, like a title issue, a seller pushing back on staging choices, or a vendor falling through, do they have something to reference, or do they call you?

## Section D - Seller Communication

**Q019** (Financial Accountability) Manus flag: Restricted
Before: When a seller pushes back on feedback or asks about a price adjustment, can they handle that conversation and land on next steps without looping you in first?
After: When a seller pushes back on feedback or has a question that's outside their lane, can they navigate that moment and get to the right next step, whether that means handling it directly or routing it to you, without you having to manage it personally?

**Q021** (Financial Accountability) Manus flag: Depends
Before: If a seller ends up upset about how a conversation with them went, do they work it out directly, or does it end up needing your involvement?
After: If a seller gets upset about a scheduling issue or a communication gap, do they smooth that over directly, or does it end up needing your involvement?

**Q022** (Role Accountability) Manus flag: Depends
Before: In the last 30 days, how often did they handle a tough seller conversation, like declining feedback or a stalled showing schedule, on their own?
After: In the last 30 days, how often did they handle a tough seller conversation on their own, like a stalled showing schedule or a seller frustrated about turnaround time?

**Q024** (Role Accountability) Manus flag: Restricted
Before: When a seller's expectations need to be reset, like after weeks with no offers, do they bring you a plan for that conversation, or do they ask you how to handle it?
After: When a seller starts asking why there's been no activity after weeks on market, do they bring you a plan for addressing it, like refreshed marketing or updated photos, or do they ask you how to handle it?

**Q027** (Systems and Checklists) Manus flag: Restricted
Before: If a seller conversation gets difficult, like a price reduction discussion, do they have talking points or a guide to work from, or do they call you first?
After: If a seller conversation gets difficult, like frustration over a lack of showings, do they have talking points or a guide to work from, or do they call you first?

**Q034** (Culture and Communication) Manus flag: Depends
Before: How confident are you that they can manage the full seller relationship, updates and difficult conversations included, without you?
After: How confident are you that they can manage the full day-to-day seller relationship, including tense moments about scheduling or communication gaps, without you?

## Section E - File Opening

**Q045** (Systems and Checklists) Manus flag: Restricted
Before: If a file has something unusual, like a contingency that's not standard, do they have a reference for that, or do they call you?
After: If a file has something unusual, like extra documentation needed for a nonstandard contingency, do they know how to flag it and gather what's needed, or do they call you?

## Section F - Lender Tracking

**Q060** (Role Accountability) Manus flag: Depends
Before: When a lending delay threatens the closing date, do they bring you a plan to address it, or do they ask you what to do?
After: When a lending delay threatens the closing date, do they bring you options for rescheduling logistics, like updated timing with title and the other side's office, or do they ask you what to do?

**Q063** (Systems and Checklists) Manus flag: Restricted
Before: If a lending issue is unusual, like an appraisal coming in low, do they have a playbook for that, or do they call you?
After: If a lending issue is unusual, like a delayed underwriting condition, do they have a playbook for gathering what's needed and notifying the right people, or do they call you?

## What stayed the same in every rewrite

Each question still tests the identical construct the dimension is supposed to measure:
independence, proactivity, documentation, or confidence. Only the concrete scenario
changed, from one that requires a license to one that doesn't. Nothing about the answer
options, the scoring formula, or the dimension weights needs to change alongside this.
