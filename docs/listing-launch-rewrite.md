# Ownership Debt Assessment — Listing Launch Rewrite (Sample)
## Mode B / Full OQI question set — Section C

This is a sample rewrite of the 18 Mode B questions for Listing Launch only (Section C), meant to be reviewed before applying the same approach to Seller Communication, File Opening, and Lender Tracking.

What changed and what didn't:
- Every question now references something concrete to an actual listing launch (MLS entry, CMA/pricing, staging, photography, disclosures, seller communication) instead of the generic "[Workflow]" bracket-fill.
- Dimension, weight, and scoring direction are completely unchanged. Every question still measures the exact same construct, at the exact same weight, on the same 0 to 4 scale where 0 means fully dependent on you and 4 means fully independent. This is copy-only. The OQI formula does not change.
- "The named owner" is no longer repeated in every question. Per the Manus research, no established assessment dynamically pipes in a name, so instead of adding a name-capture field, the fix is simpler: establish who "they" refers to once, at the top of the section (e.g. "You told us your Transaction Coordinator owns Listing Launch. The next 18 questions are about how that's actually going."), then use natural pronouns throughout instead of restating "the named owner of the workflow" 18 times.

## Rewritten questions

### Financial Accountability (weight 0.20 total, 0.07 per question)

Q001 From getting the listing agreement signed to the property going live on MLS, can they make the calls on things like vendor selection, staging decisions, and listing details without checking with you first?

Q002 When they need to send status updates, marketing plans, or MLS confirmations to your seller, do they send those directly, or do you review everything before it goes out?

Q003 If the MLS listing goes live with an error, like a wrong price, a missing disclosure, or bad photos, do they catch and fix it themselves, or does it land back on your desk?

### Role Accountability (weight 0.22 total, 0.07 per question)

Q004 In the last 30 days, how often did they make a call on something like staging, timeline, or listing details and just move forward with it?

Q005 When a seller calls with a last-minute change, like new photos needed or a showing conflict, who fields that call first?

Q006 When something comes up mid-launch, like a delayed photographer or a seller who wants changes, do they bring you a plan, or do they bring you the problem?

### Systems and Checklists (weight 0.18 total, 0.06 per question)

Q007 Is there a written, step-by-step process for taking a listing from signed agreement to live on MLS that they actually follow?

Q008 Are the emails and updates that go out during a listing launch, like confirmations, seller updates, and marketing recaps, already templated, or written fresh each time?

Q009 If something unusual comes up, like a title issue, a seller pushing back on staging choices, or a vendor falling through, do they have something to reference, or do they call you?

### Execution and Training (weight 0.15 total, 0.05 per question)

Q010 Do they know exactly which listing launch decisions are theirs to make versus which ones need your sign-off?

Q011 In the last 60 days, did they bring you something about a listing launch that was actually within their own call to make?

Q012 If they were out for a day mid-launch, is there someone else who knows enough to keep the listing moving toward MLS?

### Outcome Accountability (weight 0.15 total, 0.05 per question)

Q013 When a listing launch goes sideways, like a missed MLS deadline, an upset seller, or a listing error, who's actually accountable to you for fixing it?

Q014 Do they have a real standard they're held to for how a listing launch goes? Not just whether the steps got done, but whether it actually went well for the seller.

Q015 If listing launches under their watch keep falling short, is there an actual process for correcting that, or does it just get absorbed?

### Culture and Communication (weight 0.10 total, 0.03 per question)

Q016 How confident are you, honestly, that they could run a listing launch start to finish without you checking in?

Q017 Watching how they operate, not just what they say, how sure do they seem when handling a listing launch on their own?

Q018 Have they actually run listing launches independently, at your standard, for 90 days or more?

## Answer options: worked examples confirming scoring stays intact

Options are reworded only enough to match the tightened question phrasing. The underlying 0 to 4 meaning and direction is identical to the original.

Q001 (was: "authority to make decisions... without checking with you first?")

| Value | Label |
|---|---|
| 0 | No, every decision comes to me first |
| 1 | Rarely, most decisions still need my sign-off |
| 2 | Sometimes, it depends on the decision |
| 3 | Usually, only the bigger calls come to me |
| 4 | Yes, full authority through the whole launch |

Q013 (was: "who is accountable to you for the outcome?")

| Value | Label |
|---|---|
| 0 | It always comes back to me to fix |
| 1 | Mostly me, with some help from them |
| 2 | Shared, we usually sort it out together |
| 3 | Mostly them, I stay in the loop |
| 4 | Fully them, they own the fix start to finish |

Q016 (was: "how confident are you...?")

| Value | Label |
|---|---|
| 0 | Not confident at all |
| 1 | A little, I'd still want to check in often |
| 2 | Somewhat, I'd check in occasionally |
| 3 | Fairly confident, I'd rarely need to step in |
| 4 | Fully confident, they'd run it better than I would |

The remaining 15 questions get the same treatment (same scale, same direction, just matched wording).
